1981

The Exploited

The Exploited is a legendary Scottish punk band formed in 1979 by vocalist Wattie Buchan in Edinburgh. Known for their aggressive sound, politically charged lyrics, and fierce anti-establishment stance, they became one of the most influential bands of the punk movement’s second wave. Their debut album, Punk’s Not Dead (1981), was a raw, unfiltered call-to-arms that pushed back against the commercialized direction punk was taking, quickly earning them a dedicated following. Their early albums, Troops of Tomorrow (1982) and Let’s Start a War (1983), cemented their reputation as incendiary voices in the punk world. With tracks like “Dead Cities” and “Alternative,” they tackled issues of social decay, class struggle, and government oppression. The Exploited’s chaotic, intense live shows often led to confrontations with police and authorities, only fueling their appeal to the disillusioned youth of the time. By the mid-1980s, The Exploited began to integrate thrash metal influences into their music, a bold crossover that paved the way for the punk-metal fusion movement. Albums like Horror Epics (1985) and Death Before Dishonour (1987) showcased this evolution, expanding their fanbase across the globe. Despite frequent lineup changes and Buchan’s health challenges, The Exploited has continued to perform and release music sporadically, with Fuck the System (2003) being their latest album. Known for their uncompromising attitude and commitment to punk’s rebellious spirit, The Exploited remain a staple in the punk world, inspiring generations of punk and hardcore bands worldwide.
